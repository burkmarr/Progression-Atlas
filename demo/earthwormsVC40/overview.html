Earthworm by Charlie Bell
The FSC Tomorrow's Biodiversity project is working in association with the Earthworm Society of Britain (ESB) to promote earthworm recording in the UK. We have produced this online mapping tool that presents the distribution maps for the vice county of Shropshire (VC40). The main purpose is to enable current recorders to target their efforts on the ground in order to fill in the gaps.

The records for these maps are periodically downloaded from the NBN Gateway. Most of the earthworm records on the NBN Gateway are publicly accessible. The most important datasets are those supplied by the ESB. These are fully publicly accessible at full resolution. On these maps it is possible to view records from ESB datasets on their own. Records from the ESB are verified.

This dialog includes instructions on using the mapping tool but, for the most part, you can learn about it just by playing with it.

If you have ideas on how this tool could be improved to give you more useful information as a contributor to earthworm recording, please let us know: admin@tombio.uk.

Anyone can get involved in recording earthworms in the UK. If you want to find out more about getting involved, contact Keiron Brown.
